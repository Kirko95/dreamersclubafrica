Reasons To Join:

  1. PARENTS WISH THEY HAD THIS FINANCIAL HEAD START
    Imagine if you had been taught at a tender age about money and, more specifically, that the way to wealth is through cash flowing assets. How different would your life be today if you had learned these concepts at an early age?
  2. THE BUCK STOPS WITH THE PARENT
    Today’s parents need to understand that psychologically, financial literacy foundation is developed as early as age 3, mainly through a child observing his/her surrounding. A parent who does not take charge to ensure the right behavior is developed, simply hands over that responsibility to other powerful forces such as media adverts to do that work for them. These adverts instill ‘buy’ habits subconsciously. It has always been, will always be the parent’s/caregiver’s decision, knowingly or unknowingly, what the foundation will be. Click here to learn more on this.
  3. DEVELOPING THE RIGHT FINANCIAL MIND SET
    Kids are like sponges. From the time they are babies, they absorb what they see and hear around them. They get their ideas about money from their environment and this solidifies to a habit. Without the right mind set at tender years, they will struggle as adults to break from the solidified wrong mindset - psychologically.
  4. THEY LEARN ‘HOW’ TO THINK, NOT ‘WHAT’ TO THINK
    When we teach kids “A” is for apple, in effect, we converge their thinking and creativity. This is teaching them, ‘what’ to think. If we ask them to come up with names that start with “A”, in effect, we diverge their thinking and creativity. This is teaching them ‘how’ to think. Our programs are designed to teach ‘how’ to think.

What We Offer

Your opportunity to give your young ones the financial head start you missed out on money management, investments & entrepreneurship with fun and engaging classroom programs at home schooling, schools, weekends and holidays.

Money management

Money management is a life skill because it is a commodity used for life. You must gain control over your money or the lack of it will forever control you. The myth that teaching children about money is not ‘good’ leads to the later. Evidence is out in the open with most adults struggling with personal finance. Like other skills fostered from a tender age such as music, football, etc money management should not be expected..

Power of The Mind

Before you can transform your wallet from poor to rich, you have to transform your mind from poor to rich. The size of your success is measured by the strength of your desire; the size of your dream; and how you handle disappointments along the way. Failure defeats losers. Failure inspires winners. These and more are at the center of the mind – subconscious and unconscious.

Investments

Investments are about making money work for you and not vice versa. The secret to real financial freedom or the way to wealth is through cash flowing assets. The philosophy of the rich and the poor is this: the rich invest their money and spend what is left. The poor spend their money and invest what is left.

Entrepreneurship

We cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them. Inside every problem lies an opportunity. Therefore, innovation is at the center of true entrepreneurship. Most people never get wealthy simply because they are not trained to recognize opportunities right in front of them.

Teaching Kids On Money Managment, Investments and Entrepreneurship

These three are deeply embedded in our daily lives that they have become life skills. By teaching kids and teens early on about how the real world works, we empower them with these life skills. Most parents already know how important it is to teach their kids & teens these skills. But there’s a big difference between knowing and taking action.

Jackline Kamau – Parent at PCEA Township Kitengela

I had just finished picking my last item at the supermarket and as my 12 year old daughter was pushing this large trolley towards the counter, she asked “Mum, why do you pay for me classes at Dreamers Club Africa?” “What do you mean? It’s because I want you to learn how to take care of your money.” I responded without thinking. The next question sent shivers down my spine. “You have a full trolley and most of these stuffs are wants you don’t really require. For instance, why have you picked three perfumes and at home I am sure you have like two? Don’t you think you are wasting money?” I thought I understood the value of these classes until that point. I was forced to reflect on my own behaviors. I have to admit, it is a struggle but I am making small progress.

An eye opening truth - Why most adults have unhealthy personal Habit

Part 1 - Personal Finance Statistics in Kenya

This is the first blog for Dreamers Club Africa and it aims to point out why most adults, especially in Africa, have unhealthy personal finance habits.

When a society has widespread unhealthy personal finance habits or a lack of financial literacy, that society is very likely to face economic and social crises. It is therefore important that we appreciate the broader impacts of personal finance rather than just focus on looking at it in an individualistic way.

It is said that numbers don’t lie and in a world that appreciates the power of data more than ever, we can’t ignore the facts Before setting up Dreamers Club Africa, we were concerned about how Kenyans appear to struggle with personal finance despite being well educated. We decided to first do some research on the financial literacy level in the country across various demographic groups. We targeted retirees who had prime jobs during their working days and the current working nation.

The statistics were alarming; 95% of retirees heavily relied on their pension and upkeep from their loved ones for survival. All of the retirees had tried some form of business ventures but over 93% were unsuccessful, hence the dependency on their pension and loved ones. About 97% attributed their difficulties to poor knowledge on money management, investment decisions and a lack of entrepreneurship skills . More interestingly, the retirees were more concerned about the working nation than their own current status.According to them, the working nation is walking down the very same path they did. The working nation is on a path that leads to the same financial difficulties retirees face today. They say whenever they try to advise or warn the working nation, it appears to fall on deaf ears.

The working nation were asked two simple questions:‘“if you had the financial literacy and knowledge you have today before you started working, how different do you think your life would be today?”’. 100% responded by saying their lives would have been way better, signaling ‘regret’ or a ‘ wish to have had that knowledge earlier in their lives’. The second question was: ‘“given what you have learnt so far, what have you done or are you doing, to ensure your children gain that knowledge before they start working?’”Approximately 93% had not done anything about it or had never thought of it this way. About 3% responded by saying, that they believed traditional schooling for their children would address the issue.When asked why they did not have that knowledge themselves having gone to school, they had no response.

The lingering question is this:, why would the working nation,majority of whom are parents, feel a sense of regret over their limited financial knowledge but not do anything to ensure their children follow a better path .After doing some more research, we were able to unlock the reason and this will be the turning point going forward for parents with an open mind. The answer liesin the psychology at play of how the subconscious and conscious mind work. This is covered in part two.
